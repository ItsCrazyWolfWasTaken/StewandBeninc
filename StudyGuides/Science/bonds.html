Bonds Unit

      Valence Electrons

    • Valence electrons are the outermost shell of an atom.
    • Octet Rule: A complete and stable shell requires eight valence e except for hydrogen and helium, which require 2.
    • Noble gases typically do not react because they have a full valence shell.

      Ionic Bonding

    • Ionic bonding involves the transfer of electrons from one atom to another, resulting in the formation of charged ions.
    • Typically, metals lose e to form cations, while nonmetals gain electrons to form anions.
    • Oxidation numbers indicate the charge of an ion.

      Lewis Dot Structures

    • To create place dots around the element's symbol to show amount of Valence electrons
    • Draw arrows to show transfer of electrons

      Covalent Bonds

    • Covalent bonding involves the sharing of e between two nonmetals, allowing both atoms to achieve a full valence shell.
    • In a single bond, two e are shared
    • In a double bond four e are shared
    • In a triple bond six e are shared.

      Naming Chemical Formulas

    • The first element retains its name
    • The second element's name ends in ide.
    • Mono, Di, Tri, Tetra are added to signify amount
    • Mono is lost in the first word.
